## The Challenge

A national solar company was stuck at 28 deals per month. The marketing team was spending six figures on Google Ads and Facebook but had no clear visibility into which sources were actually converting. The sales team was drowning in low-quality leads and missing follow-ups left and right.

The CRM was a mess. No automations. No lead scoring. No real pipeline visibility. Reps were cherry-picking leads and letting hundreds go cold.

## The Approach

We rebuilt the entire marketing-to-sales system from scratch.

**Paid Media Overhaul:** Restructured Google Ads campaigns around high-intent keywords and geographic performance data. Cut underperforming ad groups. Built new landing pages for each service area with localized copy and social proof.

**CRM & Automation Build:** Stood up a full GoHighLevel instance with automated lead routing, SMS/email sequences, appointment booking, and pipeline tracking. Every lead got touched within 60 seconds of submission.

**Sales Enablement:** Built real-time dashboards in Looker Studio so managers could see setter and closer performance daily. Designed new comp plans that aligned incentives with actual deal quality, not just appointment volume.

**Lead Source Optimization:** Implemented ActiveProspect for real-time lead verification and quality scoring. Started tracking cost-per-sale by source instead of just CPL. This changed everything.

## The Results

- **28 to 60 deals/month** within 6 months
- **72% reduction** in cost-per-sale ($4,976 down to $1,363)
- **3x improvement** in lead-to-appointment conversion rate
- **Zero manual follow-up** -- every lead hits an automated sequence

## Key Takeaways

- Tracking cost-per-sale instead of cost-per-lead completely changes how you allocate budget. A cheap lead that never closes is the most expensive lead you can buy.
- Automation is not about replacing people. It is about making sure no lead falls through the cracks while your team focuses on the ones most likely to close.
- Comp plans drive behavior. If you are rewarding volume over quality, that is exactly what you will get.
