## The Challenge

A multi-location home services company was spending $50K/month on Google Ads with a cost-per-lead hovering around $85. They knew they were wasting money but could not figure out where. Campaign structure was a mess -- broad match keywords everywhere, one landing page for everything, and no conversion tracking beyond form submissions.

They had no idea which locations were profitable and which were burning cash.

## The Approach

**Campaign Audit & Restructure:** Broke monolithic campaigns into location-specific and service-specific ad groups. Moved from broad match to phrase and exact match with a disciplined negative keyword strategy. Set up proper conversion tracking with offline conversion imports from their CRM.

**Landing Page Overhaul:** Built dedicated landing pages for each major service and metro area. Each page had localized copy, local phone numbers, service-specific social proof, and a streamlined form. A/B tested headlines and form length.

**Bidding Strategy:** Shifted from maximize clicks to target CPA bidding once we had enough conversion data. Set different CPA targets by location based on historical close rates and average deal values.

**Negative Keyword Management:** Built a comprehensive negative keyword list and reviewed search term reports weekly. We were bleeding money on informational queries, competitor brand terms, and job-seeker traffic.

## The Results

- **40% reduction** in cost-per-lead ($85 down to $51)
- **25% increase** in lead-to-appointment rate from better landing pages
- **Profitable spend** in 8 of 10 locations (up from 4)
- **Clear attribution** by location, service, and keyword theme

## Key Takeaways

- Most Google Ads accounts are not broken. They are just disorganized. Structure alone can cut CPL by 20-30% without changing a single ad.
- If you are not tracking conversions downstream of the form fill, you are flying blind. A $50 lead that never picks up the phone costs you more than a $100 lead that books an appointment.
- Weekly search term reviews are not optional. Set a calendar reminder and do it every Monday.
