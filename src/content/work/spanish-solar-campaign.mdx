## The Challenge

The company was operating in markets with significant Spanish-speaking populations but running zero Spanish-language campaigns. Every ad, landing page, and follow-up sequence was English-only. This was not just a missed opportunity -- competitors were already moving in.

The team had no bilingual marketing assets, no Spanish-language CRM workflows, and no one on the marketing side who could build this from scratch.

## The Approach

**Market Analysis:** Identified the top 15 zip codes by Spanish-speaking population density within our existing service areas. Cross-referenced with solar adoption data and competitor activity to prioritize launch markets.

**Full Funnel Build:** Created Spanish-language Google Ads campaigns, Meta ad sets, and dedicated landing pages. This was not Google Translate -- we worked with native speakers to get the copy right. Cultural nuances matter.

**CRM Workflow:** Built parallel Spanish-language automation flows in GoHighLevel. SMS sequences, email follow-ups, appointment confirmations -- all in Spanish. Routed leads to bilingual setters.

**Landing Pages:** Built mobile-first landing pages with culturally relevant imagery, testimonials from Spanish-speaking homeowners, and financing information formatted for the audience. Added click-to-call with bilingual reps.

## The Results

- **Opened a completely new market segment** with near-zero competition
- **CPL 30% lower** than equivalent English campaigns in the same zip codes
- **Higher appointment show rates** due to native-language follow-up
- **Expanded to 3 additional markets** based on initial success

## Key Takeaways

- If your service area has a significant non-English-speaking population and you are not marketing to them in their language, you are leaving money on the table.
- Translation is not localization. The ads, landing pages, and follow-up all need to feel native. Bad translations erode trust instantly.
- Spanish-language markets often have lower CPCs and CPLs because fewer advertisers are competing for the keywords. The math works.
