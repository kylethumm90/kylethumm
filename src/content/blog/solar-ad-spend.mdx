I have audited a lot of solar companies' ad accounts over the past few years. Big national players, regional shops, local installers. The number is almost always the same: somewhere between 30-50% of their ad spend is generating zero revenue.

Not low revenue. Zero.

Here is where the money goes to die.

## The Brand Term Black Hole

Most solar companies are bidding on their own brand name without realizing it. Your SEO already puts you at the top for "your company name solar." When you also run ads on those terms, you are paying $3-8 per click for traffic that was already coming to you for free.

Check your search term report right now. Filter by your company name and variations of it. I bet you find thousands of dollars in wasted spend.

The fix is simple. Add your brand terms as negative keywords in your non-brand campaigns. If you want to run brand campaigns defensively (to block competitors), fine -- but do it intentionally with a separate budget, not accidentally mixed into your prospecting campaigns.

## The "Solar" Keyword Trap

Here is a keyword that looks great on paper: "solar panels." High volume. Relevant. Seems like a no-brainer.

Except most people searching "solar panels" are doing research. They are not ready to get a quote. They want to know how solar panels work, how much they cost in general, what the pros and cons are. Your $15 click just went to someone who is 6-12 months away from making a decision.

The keywords that actually convert are specific and intent-driven: "solar installation quote [city]", "solar company near me", "how much does solar cost for my house." These get fewer searches but the people clicking them are ready to talk to someone.

## The Follow-Up Failure

This is not technically an ad spend problem, but it turns your ad spend into waste. You spend $5,000 on Google Ads. You generate 100 leads. Your setters call each lead once, maybe twice, over the next 48 hours. They reach maybe 30 of them. The other 70 leads go cold.

You just paid $3,500 for nothing.

The solution is automation. Every lead should get an immediate text. Then an email. Then another text 2 hours later. Then a call. Then another sequence the next day. GoHighLevel makes this trivial to set up.

Most solar companies do not have a lead generation problem. They have a lead follow-up problem. Fix the follow-up before you increase the ad spend.

## The Math That Matters

Stop tracking cost-per-lead. Start tracking cost-per-sale.

A $50 lead that converts at 2% costs you $2,500 per sale. A $100 lead that converts at 8% costs you $1,250 per sale. The "expensive" lead source is actually half the price.

When you start looking at your ad spend through this lens, the 40% waste becomes obvious. You will find sources generating cheap leads that never close and expensive leads that close all day long. Reallocate accordingly.
